About Weavers Day Out

At Weavers Day Out, we believe education goes beyond books.

Since 2024, We are dedicated to preserving India’s handloom heritage by introducing young minds to the art of weaving. Through our one-day guided trips, students get to see how beautiful Ikat fabrics are made, understand village life, and learn valuable lessons about tradition, teamwork, and creativity.

Our program is designed with safety, fun, and learning in mind – ensuring students enjoy every moment while teachers and schools benefit from a structured educational outing.

Why bhoodhan pochampally?

• Bhoodhan Pochampally is a historic village in Telangana, often called the “Silk City of India.

• It is world-renowned for Pochampally Ikat, a unique style of tie-and-dye weaving that creates stunning geometric patterns.

• The handloom products from here, especially sarees, have earned Geographical Indication (GI) tag recognition.

• In 1951, the village became famous for the Bhoodhan (Land Donation) Movement, started by Acharya Vinoba Bhave, where landlords donated land to the poor — a movement that began here and spread across India.

• In 2021, the UN World Tourism Organization recognized Pochampally as one of the “Best Tourism Villages in the World.”

• This makes Pochampally not only a craft hub but also a place of history, culture, and inspiration.
